For a long while, I had been in pursuit of freedom—of the greatest life I could imagine for myself—redefining, at every turn, the very terms of victory so that the chase might never end.

I sought the liberation promised by the invisible hand, by that divine and hidden order of the cosmos; I had long resolved never to become a slave to causality, never to be driven into a life hemmed in by restriction and limit, and so I gave myself over instead to the pursuit of arbitrary goals, of endless wealth and success.

I was running after a spectre that promised deliverance.

The hidden motions of the world have led me here, to this moment.

I have seen much and done much, and still it was never enough.

I had forgotten the beauty that permeates everything.

It is time to lay down arms.

It is time to live now.

---

There is beauty in the truth: that the ideals of discovery, freedom, and knowledge carry both the individual and the whole of history toward a culminating freedom.

The spirit of man is the spirit of history.

Now I no longer run from the past.

I run toward the future.
