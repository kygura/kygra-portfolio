100m: 10 seconds for the performance of a lifetime.

---

I was a promising short-distance runner. Natural born, insanely fast for my age, outcompeting most of my peers with barely any effort. Delusional. Felt protected by the gods and destined for greatness. 

Not for glory, fame, or wealth. Not to justify existence. But for the sake of finding something and giving it my whole body and soul. And yet — the first signs of resistance, the friction of the routine and the training, left me bored. I simply moved on. Without feeling or hesitation. I just stopped caring.

Perhaps that is a philosophy and lifestyle all of my own. A jack of all trades who has been everywhere and done most things. Nothing to prove, nothing to lose.

Hard to beat the fraud, larper, wasted potential allegations.

---

I lack the soul of commitment others have. I have had this exploratory mindset — over-diversifying my presence and portfolio so as to not commit to dead or wasteful dreams.

Even calling it a dream feels like a stupid gimmick. Dreams die all the time. For me, though, they just change shape. All the things I did, I did them teleologically — for their own sake. I never bought the fantasy of the unyielding dream. I yield. Everybody does.

I escaped reality. My terms are my own.

Freedom becomes costly. Paid dearly in the sacrifice of many futures unseen. To be enveloped in freedom does not liberate you from its consequences.

**The price of this euphoric effortless freedom is paid for with your own life. What is sacrificed cannot be seen, only felt.**

---

A life of ultimate single focus would have been its own kind of liberation. The specialist's liberation is depth. Mine is surface area. Both are real. Both cost something. But only one survives regime change.

The phantom pain lingers. Not as grief for lost futures, but as the felt weight of carrying something that never found its final shape — because it was never supposed to. It just moves.

And yet — optionality was the wildcard factor that gave me this lifestyle, this perspective, and made it the winning strategy for this particular timeline and life. The same pattern I interrogate as a flaw is also the reason I am here — location-independent, multi-domain, adaptable, unowned.

The jack of all trades survived. The specialist might have peaked and cratered in a single market cycle or a single injury.

---

In this scenario, winning must be a side product of greatness. Not pursued by itself. The moment you optimize for the win directly, you corrupt the thing that produces it. Greatness as the process, winning as the exhaust.

---

There is strength in the refusal of allowing circumstances to impose their will and define you. The awareness that reality could have dealt you a different hand and the outcome would have been the same — because your will was the constant.

The domains were never the point. They were just surfaces the will moved across.

---

It is very unclear to me what the true shape of my soul is. In the pathways of souls, the crossroads of all roads for men of great destinies to meet — I cannot imagine what transfiguration, what permutation of a man's soul, lies beyond the veil of that particular unknown, never to be known to me.

The men of great destinies I imagine at that crossroads were not standing there surveying. They were already gone. Down one road. Blind to the others. Not because they were braver, but because they could not see what I see. Their limitation was their passage.

My gift — the panoramic vision, the refusal to be owned — is also my wall.

---

I have no idea what path I can reforge ahead of me. My moves are limited, expanded, and redefined by the constitution of my character.

The phantom pain lingers.

Venom Snake type feeling.

All I know is I can't give up.

That is enough.

---

## The Sprinter's Soul

The mechanics of the sprint: a sudden burst of explosive performance. Quick muscle fibers. Not much different from who I am — a reflection of the life I lead.

Explosive output, fast-twitch dominance, recovery, next event. That is not just how I ran — it is how I have moved through everything. Music, trading, writing, travel. Short bursts of intense performance, then gone.

Fast-twitch is not a deficiency. It is a fiber type. You do not train a sprinter into a marathoner without destroying what makes them fast. The body rebels. The architecture is wrong for it.

The question was never "why can't I commit long-term." It was always "what does commitment look like for someone built like this." The answer is not grinding daily for years in one lane. It is repeated explosions, strategically aimed, with recovery between them. Periodized. Like a sprinter's training block.

The only adaptation needed: build a structure that compounds across the explosions instead of losing everything between them. Infrastructure that catches the output of each burst and holds it while you recover.

Sprint. Build the thing that remembers what you did. Sprint again.

---

## The Synthesis

Do the thing for its own sake, but don't leave when it gets boring. The greatness-first orientation plus the refusal to quit. The winning follows or it doesn't. Either way, the soul stays intact.

The will was always the constant. Not the runner, not the trader, not the builder. The will underneath all of them. The thing that survived every exit, every pivot, every clean abandonment. The potential was never in sprinting or any single path. It was in the engine itself. And the engine is still running.

A life condensed in 100m. In 10s. The most beautiful and brutal metaphor. An entire existence compressed into a single line — and then it's over. That is the life I did not live. But I chose the long race instead. No finish line, no clock, no lane. Just open ground.

A sprinting soul.

## The Direction

Most in this sport run from something — from their past.

I am running towards something. Towards a greater future. No looking back. The slowness of things will not stall me.

The distinction matters. Running from is reactive, governed by fear, shaped by what you leave behind. Running towards is volitional — the will aimed forward, unconditioned by origin.

The sprint is not escape. It is pursuit.

I outrun reality. Reality cannot hold us in place.

Most in this sport run from something — from their past. I am running towards something. Towards a greater future. No looking back. The slowness of things will not stall me.

A sprinting soul.

## The Fire

I have long sought after this fire, this fast-twitch muscle fiber which makes up the fabric of my soul and permeates my being. My ideas and execution have lost their vision and edge. A confused performance and a shallow pantomime of expertise.

I have the guts though.

Most in this sport run from something — from their past. I am running towards something. Towards a greater future. No looking back. The slowness of things will not stall me.

The fire is not gone. It is dispersed. Too many surfaces, not enough concentrated heat. The fast-twitch system works, but it needs fewer targets and harder sprints. Not more commitment in the marathon sense — more intensity in the burst sense. Fewer things, done at full violence.

The guts are the foundation. The rest is just aim.
